12-Tone Equally Tempered Music Scale

Octave is the interval between two musical notes whose frequencies (pitches) have ratio of 1 to 2.
Octave (a group consisting of eight items, 8ve or 8va) is divided to 8 intervals or notes. The notes are given 7 letter names: A, B, C, D, E, F, G. The major scale or mode is one of diatonic scales and it consist of eight notes: seven and first note from the following octave. The diatonic scale (Greek: διατεινειν; to stretch out) is a scale whose notes are on the lines and the spaces of the staff. The tonic is the first note of a musical scale. In case of C-major scale, the tonic is note C.

The C-major scale

In musical tuning called 12-Tone Equal Temperament (12-TET) scale, which is an example of chromatic scale (Greek: χρωμα; color - originally the additional notes are written in color), the octave is divided to 12 equal intervals (semitones). Each consecutive semitone has pitch 2(1/12) times higher. This scale is used predominantly in Western music to tune different instruments like guitar or MIDI-keyboard. The additional 5 notes have frequency (pitch) lower or higher by a semitone. The postfix es or symbol flat b is used for tones lower than a note and the postfix is or symbol sharp # for tones with raised pitch.

The cent [c] is relative unit of measurement on logarithmic scale. One cent is equal to one hundredth part of the logarithm of the ratio of the frequencies of the two consecutive and equally tempered semitones (notes).
100R = log2(B/A) [c], therefore one octave has 1200 cents.
